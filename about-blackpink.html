About BLACKPINK

BLACKPINK is more than just a K-pop group — they’re a global sensation that has taken the music world by storm. Formed by YG Entertainment, BLACKPINK debuted in 2016, and since then, they've redefined the landscape of modern pop music with their bold sound, powerhouse performances, and trendsetting influence.

Comprising members Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa, BLACKPINK brings together diverse cultural backgrounds and unique talents that contribute to their unparalleled success. Their music fuses hip-hop, pop, EDM, and R&B in a way that resonates worldwide. Whether it's their powerful vocals, cutting-edge fashion, or larger-than-life stage presence, BLACKPINK constantly breaks boundaries.

From topping charts to setting records, BLACKPINK’s impact stretches far beyond music. Their collaborations with global artists, sold-out stadium tours, and presence in pop culture have made them icons of the entertainment industry.

BLACKPINK is a revolution in music and pop culture.

Global Impact

BLACKPINK is the first K-pop girl group to perform at Coachella, a groundbreaking moment in K-pop history. Their music videos are among the most-viewed in YouTube history, and their influence can be seen across industries including fashion, entertainment, and even social media trends.

Record-Breaking Success

From their debut single "BOOMBAYAH" to their latest releases, BLACKPINK has shattered records worldwide. Their song “Ddu-Du Ddu-Du” holds the title for the most-viewed music video by a K-pop group on YouTube, and they are the first K-pop girl group to have over a billion streams on Spotify.
